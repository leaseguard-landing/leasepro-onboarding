High-Level Overview

What the LeasePro Liability Waiver Program is

LeasePro is LeaseGuard's Liability Waiver Program. It helps the property management company or ownership group manage resident-caused property damage exposure when a resident does not provide compliant renters insurance. It gives residents a clear choice: participate in the LeasePro liability waiver workflow or opt out by uploading compliant renters insurance through the ManageGo portal.

  • Provides a consistent liability waiver program process for residents who have not provided compliant renters insurance.
  • Supports the lease insurance requirement without requiring onsite teams to manually verify policies.
  • Allows approved resident policies to be verified and monitored by LeaseGuard.
  • Creates a repeatable process for move-ins, renewals, lapses, cancellations, and expirations.
Important: LeasePro is not renters insurance for residents, not a personal lines policy, and does not make the resident an insured. Residents who want personal renters insurance must buy their own policy and upload it for verification.
Team Summary

What property teams need to know

Onsite teams should use simple, consistent language with residents. Their role is to explain the lease requirement, direct residents to the ManageGo portal, and follow the approved process.

  • Residents may participate in the LeasePro Liability Waiver Program or opt out by uploading compliant renters insurance.
  • Residents must use the ManageGo portal for verification.
  • LeaseGuard reviews the policy; onsite teams should not approve or deny policies manually.
  • If a policy fails verification or later lapses, cancels, or expires, the LeasePro liability waiver workflow can apply.
  • Current-month charge handling should follow the approved program timing and accounting rules.
LeasePro Coverage Overview

Who is insured, what causes of loss are included, and what limit applies

This is a property-team training summary. Client-specific policy forms, endorsements, limits, sublimits, deductibles, and state requirements should be confirmed in the final program documents.

Who is insured

  • The property management company, ownership group, or related property entity shown as the insured in the program documents.
  • The resident is not insured under the waiver and should not be told they are buying renters insurance.
  • Claims are handled through the approved LeasePro Liability Waiver Program and should be submitted by the property management team.

Coverage limits

  • The LeasePro Liability Waiver Program coverage limit is $100,000 per occurrence.
  • The required limit should be reflected consistently in the lease addendum, resident communications, accounting setup, and onboarding materials.
  • Any deductibles, sublimits, exclusions, and special conditions should be reviewed in the final policy and client onboarding materials.

Covered causes of loss

  • Fire.
  • Smoke.
  • Explosion originating in the resident premises.
  • Falling objects that damage or penetrate the roof or wall of the resident premises.
  • Riot or civil commotion.
  • Overflow, discharge, or leakage of water in the resident premises from a bath, sink, shower, toilet, clothes washing machine, dishwasher, refrigerator, other appliance owned by the resident, aquarium, or waterbed.
  • Freezing of pipes when tenant caused.
  • Loss of use/additional living expense and loss of rental income, when included by the approved program terms.
  • Sprinkler leakage.
  • Bed bug coverage.
  • Fungi, mold, or bacteria coverage.
Program positioning: LeasePro helps protect the property against covered resident-caused loss. It does not replace the resident's personal renters insurance for personal property, relocation benefits owed to the resident, or resident personal liability outside the approved program terms.

Resident Messaging

How to explain it, and what not to say

Leasing teams should use approved, neutral language. They should explain the lease requirement and the resident's options, not sell or advise on personal insurance.

Use this type of language

  • "Your lease requires liability insurance. You can satisfy that requirement through the LeasePro Liability Waiver Program or by uploading compliant renters insurance through the ManageGo portal."
  • "LeaseGuard verifies uploaded policies for compliance with the property requirements."
  • "If your policy is approved, it will be monitored for lapse, cancellation, or expiration."
  • "If your policy does not meet requirements or later becomes non-compliant, the LeasePro waiver fee may apply."

Do not say this

  • Do not call LeasePro renters insurance.
  • Do not tell residents they are buying an insurance policy through the property.
  • Do not tell residents their personal belongings are covered by LeasePro.
  • Do not approve, deny, or interpret resident policies at the site level.
  • Do not promise claim outcomes, coverage decisions, or refunds outside the approved process.
Claims

Claims process for property teams

Claims should be filed by a property management team member, not by the resident. The property team should gather documentation and submit the claim through the ManageGo portal.

  • Open the LeasePro claims workflow in the ManageGo portal.
  • Enter the date of loss, property name, unit number, resident name, description of loss, and property contact.
  • Upload incident details, photos, repair invoices, and supporting documentation.
  • Watch for claim status updates and requests for additional information through the approved workflow.
  • Contact LeaseGuard support if a claim needs escalation or routing assistance.
Have ready: date of loss, policy number, insured name, details of loss, photos, invoices, and property team contact information.
Staff FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is LeasePro renters insurance?

No. LeasePro is LeaseGuard's Liability Waiver Program. It is not renters insurance, not a personal lines insurance policy, and does not make the resident an insured.

Are residents insured under the waiver?

No. The resident is not insured under the waiver. The property management company, ownership group, or related property entity is the insured and beneficiary of the tenant legal liability coverage for the unit.

What are the resident benefits?

Residents benefit because the coverage helps protect the insured from covered resident-caused property damage, which can reduce the need for the insured to pursue subrogation or recovery against the resident for covered damages. The LeasePro Liability Waiver Program also helps residents maintain compliance with their lease insurance requirement when they have not provided qualifying renters insurance.

Can a resident opt out of the waiver?

Yes. A resident can opt out anytime during the lease term by providing proof of qualifying renters insurance through the ManageGo portal. The associated waiver fee is ended starting the following month as long as the resident maintains qualifying coverage.

When a resident opts out of the waiver, will they get a refund?

No. No refunds or prorates are administered when a resident opts out. Once a resident successfully opts out, the waiver fee is ended from the resident ledger starting the following month.

What is LeaseGuard verifying?

LeaseGuard reviews the uploaded policy against the approved requirements, which may include liability limit, policy status, effective dates, resident name, property address, and other client-specific requirements.

What is LeaseGuard monitoring?

LeaseGuard monitors approved resident policies for lapse, cancellation, expiration, and other compliance issues that may cause the resident to move back into the LeasePro liability waiver workflow.

What happens if a resident's policy does not meet requirements?

The resident may be charged the LeasePro waiver fee, and the unit may be included under the approved LeasePro Liability Waiver Program according to the approved terms.

Can onsite teams remove current-month charges?

Current-month charge handling should follow the approved program rules. If a policy is successfully verified, the LeasePro waiver fee is ended starting the following month rather than through a one-off site-level exception.

Who should file a claim?

A property management team member should file the claim. Residents should not file claims directly under the approved LeasePro Liability Waiver Program.
